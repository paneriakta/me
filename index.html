How I lead

// opinions, held loosely but documented thoroughly
→ on quality

Ship-readiness is a rubric, not a feeling

When quality is a vibe, it loses every argument with a deadline. I build artifacts — readiness rubrics, decision logs — that let the team defend craft with evidence instead of adjectives.

→ on systems

If it's not in code, it's a suggestion

A design system that lives only in Figma is a mood board with governance. Tokens, components, and specs earn their keep when engineering can build from them without a meeting.

→ on teams

Structure is a kindness

Pods, clear ownership, and explicit operating playbooks aren't bureaucracy — they're how eight designers do their best work without stepping on each other or burning out.

→ on craft

Stay close enough to the canvas to smell the paint

Leadership doesn't mean abandoning the work. I still write specs, still push pixels, still prototype in code — because the best design decisions are made by people who can feel them.
